Short Bio

Anderson Uchôa is a PhD student in the Department of Informatics at PUC-Rio. The postgraduate program of the Department of Informatics is evaluated by the Brazilian Federal Agency for Postgraduate Education (CAPES) as being of international excellence (maximum score, 7). According to the Times Higher Education Latin America University Rankings 2017, PUC-Rio is one of the Top 10 universities in the continent and the leader in partnerships with industry.

He holds a Master's in Informatics from PUC-Rio (2019). Anderson holds a BSc degree in Software Engineering from Federal University of Ceará (UFC), Brazil, in cooperation with Sapienza University of Rome (La Sapienza), Italy (2016).

Anderson Uchôa is a member of the OPUS Research Group at PUC-Rio supervised by Prof. Dr. Alessandro Garcia which is part of the Software Engineering Laboratory of PUC-Rio. His main research interests are code smells, software maintenance and evolution, software reuse and mining software repositories. His research are available in publications.

Feel free to contact me. Specially if you are a researcher searching for collaboration.
